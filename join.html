How to Apply

Please get in touch with Nuno (nuno.morais@nms.unl.pt) if you are interested in our work and would like to be part of the lab. Include a brief letter of motivation describing your research interests, your CV, and the contacts of two references.

By default, candidates are expected to apply to their own fellowships (see types of opportunities below). When funded positions are available, they are announced above. We encourage prospective candidates to contact us before calls open so there is time to prepare a competitive application together.

What we look for

  • Genuine scientific curiosity, intellectual rigour, and ethical uprightness
  • Motivation to address important biomedical questions using computational approaches
  • A commitment to rigorous, reproducible, transparent, and high-quality research
  • A collaborative spirit and willingness to engage critically with ideas
  • An interest in developing computational approaches when they address genuine unmet needs in biomedical research
  • A desire to make computational methods intelligible and useful to the broader biomedical research community

We seek truth, not glory: we value reliable knowledge over fashionable results, methodological novelty for its own sake, or personal recognition.
We are not the right fit for candidates whose primary goal is to acquire technical skills as ends in themselves. We are interested in people who see computational methods as tools—whether existing or newly developed—for answering important biological questions and making biomedical research more rigorous, transparent, and accessible.

Opportunity Types
Postdoc

Postdoctoral Researchers

Should we be chosen as hosts, we are particularly happy to support the application of very promising young scientists to competitive fellowship schemes, such as:

  • La Caixa Junior Leader Fellowships
  • Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellowships
  • FCT Individual Call to Scientific Employment Stimulus
  • EMBO Postdoctoral Fellowships
  • Human Frontier Science Program Fellowships
PhD

PhD Candidates

We regularly consider applications from PhD candidates with their own funding or applying to competitive PhD fellowship programmes, including:

  • FCT Individual PhD Studentships
  • La Caixa INPhINIT Fellowships
  • Boehringer Ingelheim Fonds PhD Fellowships (international students)

Please contact us well in advance of call deadlines, not when calls are already open, to allow time for the selection process and to build a competitive application together. We also consider co-supervision of PhDs in industry.

Masters

Masters Theses

Some of our projects are compatible with Masters theses in Biomedical Research, Bioinformatics, Computational Biology, Biomedical Engineering, and related subjects. Exceptionally skilled and motivated candidates are welcome to get in touch about future academic years.

Short-term

Short-term Training & Academic Visits

We occasionally host short-term trainees and visiting scientists. Amongst potential sources of funding, we highlight the EMBO Scientific Exchange Grants. At the more junior level, we favour trainees with Bioengineering and Bioinformatics backgrounds.
