Research

We apply mostly computational approaches, particularly those involving the analysis of high-throughput genomic and transcriptomic data, to fundamental questions in biomedical research, namely in the context of cancer, neurobiology, and ageing. By integrating transcriptomic data with molecular and clinical information, we aim to identify novel regulatory mechanisms and molecular targets across three interconnected research themes.

RNA in cancer
CDKN2A in senescence and cancer
alternative splicing senescence
Members: Rita Martins-Silva, Maria Guerra, Mariana Ascensao-Ferreira (alumna), Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)
Collaborators: Maria Vivo

The CDKN2A locus encodes two tumour suppressors, p16INK4a and p14ARF, whose expression is driven by alternative promoters. This project investigated whether p16 expression, captured from transcriptomic data, can be used as a reliable marker of cellular senescence across tissues and disease contexts.

RNA in cancer
Uncovering the stressome: stress granule signature and its implication in cancer
stress granules cancer
Members: Alexandre Kaizeler, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)
Collaborators: Vanessa Morais
Learn more →

Stress granules are phase-separated biomolecular condensates that assemble under cellular stress conditions and have been implicated in cancer survival and drug resistance. This project uses large-scale transcriptomic data to define a computational "stressome" signature and characterise its clinical relevance across cancer types.

Alternative transcription of CELF2 in colorectal cancer progression and therapeutics
alternative splicing colorectal cancer
Members: Francisca Xara-Brasil, Lina Gallego (alumna, Co-PI), Marta Bica, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)

CELF2 is an RNA-binding protein whose expression and splicing are altered during colorectal cancer progression. This project maps the landscape of alternative CELF2 isoforms across tumour stages and examines whether specific isoforms represent therapeutic vulnerabilities or resistance mechanisms in colorectal cancer treatment.

RNA in cancer
Alternative splicing during oncogene-induced senescence
alternative splicing senescence
Members: Mariana Ascensao-Ferreira (alumna), Rita Martins-Silva, Alexandre Kaizeler, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (Co-PI)
Collaborators: Jesus Gil (PI)
Learn more →

Oncogene-induced senescence (OIS) is a potent tumour-suppressive barrier that arrests cell growth in response to aberrant oncogenic signalling. This project profiles the alternative splicing landscape during OIS to identify splicing events that regulate the senescence programme, contribute to the SASP, or enable cancer cells to bypass senescence.

Neuro-biology
Circadian disruption in a submarine crew: health impact of alternating shift work in confined environments
circadian rhythms shift work
Members: Daniel Marques, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)
Collaborators: Catia Reis (Co-PI)
Learn more →

Submarine duty combines prolonged light deprivation, irregular sleep–wake cycles, and chronic stress — a uniquely severe challenge for the circadian clock. Using transcriptomic profiling of crew members before, during, and after deployment, this project characterises the molecular signatures of circadian disruption and assesses downstream consequences for immune function, metabolism, and ageing.

cTRAP: candidate causal perturbations from differential gene expression data
pharmaco-transcriptomics CMap
Members: Bernardo Almeida (alumnus), Nuno Saraiva-Agostinho (alumnus), Juan Carlos Gomez Verjan (alumnus), Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)

cTRAP connects differential gene expression signatures from disease or perturbation experiments to the Connectivity Map (CMap) resource, identifying compounds, genetic knockouts, or cell contexts that produce matching or opposing transcriptomic changes — providing candidate causal or corrective perturbations for further experimental validation.

Bioinformatics
scStudio: interactive interface for single-cell transcriptomics data exploration
scRNA-seq immuno-senescence
Members: Marta Bica, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)
Collaborators: Karine Serre
Learn more →

scStudio is a modular, browser-based platform for exploratory and reproducible single-cell RNA-seq analysis. It supports workflows from raw count matrices through dimensionality reduction, clustering, and biological interpretation, and is being applied to characterise immunosenescence — the age-related decline of immune function — at single-cell resolution.

Bioinformatics
markeR: R toolkit for evaluating gene sets as phenotypic markers in transcriptomic data
gene signatures senescence bulk RNA-seq
Members: Rita Martins-Silva, Alexandre Kaizeler, Nuno Barbosa-Morais (PI)
Learn more →

markeR is an R/Bioconductor package providing statistical frameworks to evaluate whether a gene set functions as a reliable transcriptomic marker of a biological phenotype across independent datasets. Its primary application is benchmarking senescence gene signatures, enabling rigorous validation of candidate markers before clinical or experimental use.
