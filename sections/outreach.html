Can you crack the secret rule? Look at a set of monsters, figure out what they have in common, and form a hypothesis. Then test it: does your rule correctly predict every new monster? Keep refining until you find the one rule that works for all cases!

🔬 The Science Behind It

In real labs we look at gene expression signatures: patterns in which genes are switched on or off that consistently distinguish healthy cells from diseased ones. Finding that rule is exactly what Monster Scientists trains your brain to do.

Decision Trees

How does TikTok know exactly what video you want to watch next? The answer is a decision tree: an algorithm that sorts things step by step using yes/no questions. In this game you will sort animals by their features (fur? wings? how many legs?) and build your own sorting tree, discovering the logic behind modern recommendation systems along the way.

🔬 The Science Behind It

Decision trees are one of the most interpretable AI algorithms in use today: from Netflix recommendations to clinical diagnosis, a chain of simple if/then questions can reveal surprisingly rich structure in complex data.
