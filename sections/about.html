About the Lab

Decoding transcriptomes to understand disease

The Disease Transcriptomics Lab is a computational biology group led by Nuno Morais, based at NOVA Medical School in Lisbon, Portugal. The lab was originally established at the Instituto de Medicina Molecular (iMM, now GIMM) in 2015.

We are a multidisciplinary team applying computational and evolutionary approaches to understand how genome-wide changes in transcriptional and alternative splicing regulation in human tissues contribute to disease.

We are committed to open, transparent, intelligible, and reproducible science. Every tool we develop is freely available, well documented, and designed to empower non-computational scientists to understand and interrogate their own data.

We also coordinate BIOMICS, a European Union-funded Twinning project that fosters excellence in biomedical data science and helps train a new generation of skilled, critical-thinking, and ethically aware researchers capable of rigorously testing scientific hypotheses using biomedical data and soundly interpreting their results and implications.

📍 Where

NOVA Medical School, Lisbon, Portugal

🔬 How

Computational analysis of high-throughput genomic and transcriptomic data

🧬 Why

To elucidate the molecular basis of human diseases, namely cancer and ageing-related pathologies
